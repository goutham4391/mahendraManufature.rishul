BlazingNew Trails

Building India’s first Smart Truck, against all odds.

Scroll Down
March ‘14

Stuck and slow

It’s Monday morning and I’m late to work. Tailing a truck on a narrow street, I’m confronted with a daring remark. In Hindi, it means:

‘Overtake me if you have the guts, or zip it up.’ It would not be the only challenge thrown my way this day.

In A

Bigger Jam

“We need to do better than this! We’re falling behind,” protests one member of the board. My team and I had been anticipating this meeting for a while. But nothing prepared us for what was spoken next. “Two years – that’s all you have. Get us back in the game.” The ultimatum echoes in my ears long after people clear the room.

Our fledgling truck business needed a surge in sales. We had almost lost the customer’s faith. It would take nothing short of a breakthrough to win it back.

“How do we turn this around in two years?” I think to myself on my way home.

April ‘14

I huddle my team for a brainstorm.
It would be the first of hundreds in the months to come.
The task was simple: go big or go home.
Upping the marketing for our existing trucks would not do.

We needed a whole new way of doing business.

We needed a killer new product.

We needed a winner.

Brain-rackingmonths

We immerse ourselves in R&D.
Hundreds of customer meetings.
Over thousands of gallons of coffee.
Spark millions of joules of heated discussions.
We were just warming up. Every avenue of innovation is explored, abandoned and re-explored.

... .we finally zero in on a revolutionary new truck that would excite the customer.

Mahindra BLAZO - a smart truck with a solid guarantee.

The first thing we do is shave 450 kilos off our old truck. Then, we give it a bigger heart – a heart of 260 HP.

It was lighter on the outside and stronger on the inside – the perfect mix for a heavy load.

April ‘15

Next, we tackle mileage.

Our truck needed to take on any kind of load, on any kind of road. A single power train would not do. So we give it three power and torque options. “What if,” my chief engineer muses, “we fit switches on the dashboard so that the drivers can change to any mode at the click of a button?”

It was a bold idea and an innovation that no other truck in the world had. Like having multiple power trains in a single truck that could be changed on the go.

We call it the mPower FuelSmart Technology; it becomes the most remarkable feature of the Mahindra Blazo. It would enhance fuel economy by 5-8%, resulting in savings of nearly "16000 USD" per year on every truck.
Dec ‘15

A Smart Promise

The switches gave the driver unbeatable mileage, without compromising on power. But we didn’t want to just say it. We needed a rock-solid guarantee to win our customer’s faith.

So we make two sincere claims that no other truck company in India had made earlier.

Do or die

We were challenging the customer and the competition.

But most of all, we were challenging ourselves.

“Are you sure about this?” caution management.
“I hope you have warehouses for the trucks that come back,”
jibe colleagues.
“Do we really need to say both?” reason sales teams.

We tune out the cynics and remain resolute to our word. With all our collaterals in place, we wait with bated breath for the day of the launch.

February ‘16

Launch

The campaign rolls out and the phone lines open. At 3PM, we get our very first enquiry. What follows is 10,000 enquiries that week alone – five times more than the average during any campaign launch week.

“The truck has really moved the market. We are witnessing a lot of reciprocation.” Truck Dealer

Two years are up, and we make our way to the board room once again.This time, we walk in with confidence. And the numbers to back us up.

“In other brands, we get an average of 250-300km. In Mahindra’s vehicles, we get an additional 50km.” Jagjivan Singh Vade, Owner of a Transport Company

“We get very quick service.In case of a breakdown, help arrives within two to four hours of making a call to the service centre .” Prakash Thakkar, Partner – JR Roadlines

Beyond Numbers

But the most telling testimonials are the ones unsaid; numbers that don’t show in the books. We had challenged our customers to return the truck if it didn’t give them more mileage. And after 40 million kilometers, not one Blazo has been returned.

Not even one.
